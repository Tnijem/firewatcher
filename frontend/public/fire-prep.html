Wildfire preparedness

South Georgia isn't California, but a long drought + Pinelands + pasture-burn season creates real exposure. A prepared household can make the right call in 90 seconds; an unprepared one wastes 30 minutes hunting for the cat carrier and a phone charger.

The Ready, Set, Go! framework breaks preparedness into three stages. The list below adapts it for South GA.

Ready — long before there's any threat

Around the house

  • Clear pine straw and leaves from gutters, the roof, and within 5 feet of the foundation. Pine straw is South GA's #1 fuel.
  • Trim tree limbs back at least 10 feet from the chimney and any roof eaves.
  • Move firewood, propane tanks, and other fuels at least 30 feet from the house.
  • Keep grass cut short within 30 feet of structures during dry months.
  • Note your nearest hydrants and confirm your driveway can fit a fire truck (12 ft wide / 14 ft tall clearance).

Documents and household

  • Photograph or video every room — useful for insurance later.
  • Scan IDs, deeds, insurance policies, and titles. Keep digital copies in cloud storage you can access from a phone.
  • Sign up for CodeRED alerts via Lowndes County EMA, and enable Wireless Emergency Alerts on your phone.
  • Agree on an out-of-area contact person — someone every family member can text to confirm they're safe.

Set — when conditions are dangerous

A NWS Red Flag Warning, a regional drought above D2, or smoke visible nearby. This is before there's a fire near you, not after.

Go bag (one per person)

  • Three days of water (1 gal/person/day) and shelf-stable food
  • N95 or better masks (for smoke), plus eye drops
  • Phone charger + battery pack, NOAA weather radio (Midland WR-120 ~$30)
  • First-aid kit, prescriptions for one week, glasses, hearing-aid batteries
  • Cash ($100 in small bills — gas stations may lose power)
  • Change of clothes, boots, work gloves, sturdy hat
  • Pet carrier, leashes, food, medical records
  • Important docs in a waterproof folder

Around the house

  • Park vehicles facing out. Top off the tank.
  • Bring lawn furniture, doormats, propane grills inside or away from the house.
  • Close all windows and exterior doors. Leave interior doors open so firefighters can move through.
  • Move flammable furniture (couches, drapes) away from windows.

Go — leave now

Either an evacuation order is issued, or fire is visibly approaching faster than you can react. Do not wait to see what happens.

  • Wear long sleeves, long pants, leather shoes, and a cotton bandana — no synthetics (they melt to skin).
  • Take the go bag, pets, phones, and your out-of-area contact info.
  • If you have time and it's safe, turn off propane at the tank, leave a note on the door for first responders, and leave porch lights on.
  • Drive with headlights on, windows up, AC on recirculate. Don't drive through smoke if you can avoid it; if you must, slow down.
  • Tell your out-of-area contact you're leaving and where you're going.

Local resources

  • Emergencies: 911
  • Lowndes County EMA: lowndescountyga.gov
  • GA Forestry Commission burn permits: gatrees.org
  • NWS Tallahassee (covers Lowndes): weather.gov/tae
  • WALB news weather: walb.com
This page is general guidance, not professional advice. If you live somewhere with unusual exposure (mobile home, tin roof, large outbuildings), talk to your county fire marshal — they will visit your property for free and walk through specific recommendations.
