About

Firewatcher is a small wildfire situational-awareness dashboard for Hahira, GA and the surrounding counties — built so that a worried wife (Meredith) can look at one place and see whether anything is actually burning nearby.

Coverage area

The map is centered on Hahira (31.038°N, 83.369°W) and covers a roughly 75-mile radius — Lowndes, Brooks, Cook, Lanier, Echols, and Berrien counties in Georgia, plus parts of Hamilton and Madison counties in Florida. The bounding box is a little wider so satellite passes don't clip useful detections at the edges.

Where the data comes from

All sources are public, authoritative, and free. Firewatcher does not run its own sensors — it just aggregates what's already out there.

How to read the map

  • Each colored dot is a satellite hot-pixel detection. Color is recency (red = fresh, fading toward grey at 4+ days). Size is energy output.
  • The dashed circles are alert tiers: red = 25 mi (urgent), orange = 50 mi (notification), grey = 75 mi (display only).
  • The orange bars under the timeline are an activity histogram. Tap one to jump to that moment, or hit ▶ for radar-style replay.
  • Click any dot for a detailed readout — what kind of fire, how big, how confident the satellite is, and how far from home.
Important: A satellite hot-pixel is not proof of an uncontrolled wildfire. Burn piles, controlled prescribed burns, industrial flares, and even sun glint off metal roofs can trigger detections. Always check local sources (911 dispatch, Lowndes EMA, WALB) before assuming an emergency.

Open source

The code is MIT-licensed on GitHub. Configurations for the home point, alert radii, and zones live in .env; anyone can run their own copy for their own town.
